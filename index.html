Lana Del Rey’s Desire: Reinforcing and Critiquing Toxic Relationships

During our in-class discussions regarding music, domestic violence, and sexual assault, one profound criticism about singer-songwriter Lana Del Rey’s music is that it is inherently anti-feminist because the graphic narratives in her songs glorify domestic abuse. However, as stated in her own words, “I’m not glamorizing abuse,” but rather, “I’ve been honest about the challenging relationships I’ve had…That’s just how it is for many women,” as Mark Savage notes in his BBC article, “Lana Del Rey: 'I'm not glamorizing abuse’,” (Savage 2020).

Not only do I want to argue in favor of Lana’s illustrative storytelling of these toxic relationships, but I want to expound upon them as a critical reflection on the cultural dynamics of power, gender, and vulnerability in modern pop music through an analysis of four of her "controversial" songs: "Born to Die," "Ultraviolence," "Pretty When You Cry," and "Norman Fucking Rockwell.”

I believe a website works best when conveying my argument because it is informative yet interactive on a topic that is greatly interesting to me. In addition, I am always seeking to work on a coding project whenever I can, given that I am a Computer Science major.

Lana Del Rey: Purpose & Context

The world of Lana Del Rey can be described as one comprising a desire for a beloved. According to Per Bjørnar Grande's book chapter, “Desire in Lana Del Rey,” Lana’s virtues seem to be rooted on "...a total, non-moralistic, and in many ways uncompromising focus...” when it comes to desire, which may explain why she seems to glorify the “master and slave” dynamics in her songs. This is extremely important when understanding how desire fluctuates and evolves in toxic relationships in both the Western world and modern pop music in general (Grande 195-196).

In this case, descriptions of Lana’s power imbalance with dangerous men, followed by the vulnerability and the role of gender in these relationships, is pivotal when analyzing her intentions and impacts of these songs, so these will be the aspects that I will focus on in this project.
