Summary of Key Findings

Lana Del Rey’s melancholic yet calm voice when singing these songs provides a stark contrast to her lyrics, challenging the listeners to think deeper about her intentions when writing the songs. Both the romanticization and critique of her past relationships provides a uniquely real perspective on the issue; Lana is not trying to tell others that this reality is just but aims to bring awareness on the drawn-out nature of misplaced love due to cultural norms. According to Grande, Lana seems to have "...such a total, non-moralistic, and in many ways uncompromising focus...” when it comes to desire, which may explain why she seems to glorify the “master and slave” dynamics in her songs (Grande 195). However, as Kathanne W. Greene notes in “Women Singer-Songwriters as Exemplary Actors: The Music of Rape and Domestic Violence,” women such as Lana are “…carrying the message…” of the domestic violence movement by shedding light on the issues “…into wider popular culture,” even if it is a harsh reality (Greene 1).
